Places

Neuschwanstein Castle

The castle was commissioned by King Ludwig II of Bavaria starting in 1869 and was never fully completed. It was designed as a romanticized monument to medieval culture and kingship, inspired by the operas of Richard Wagner. The castle is one of Europe's most popular tourist attractions, receiving approximately 1.3 million visitors annually. It is located near Hohenschwangau Castle, where King Ludwig II spent his childhood.Google Maps

Altes Rathaus, Bamberg

This historic building is uniquely situated on an artificial island in the middle of the Regnitz River. The town hall was originally built in 1386. It is accessible by two bridges, the Obere Brücke and Untere Brücke. The building is adorned with intricate frescoes on its exterior walls. Bamberg's old town, including this landmark, is a UNESCO World Heritage site.Google Maps

Pilgrimage Church of Maria Gern

A baroque-style church in Berchtesgaden, Bavaria. Known for its scenic location with views of the Alps. The church is nestled in a high valley north of Berchtesgaden at approximately 700 meters (2,300 feet) above sea level. It features a distinctive onion dome and is set against the backdrop of the Watzmann mountain range. The current stone building was erected in the 18th century, replacing an earlier wooden chapel, and is a popular destination for both pilgrims and tourists. The area is well-known for photography, offering a tranquil landscape and beautiful hiking opportunities.Google Maps

Bavarian Alps

The landscape is near Schwangau, a popular tourist destination. The famous Neuschwanstein Castle is visible in the distance, nestled among the mountains. The area is characterized by lush green fields, a body of water (Forggensee), and the majestic Alps in the background. The region is known for its castles built by King Ludwig II, including Neuschwanstein, which was built in the 19th century in a romanticized medieval style.Google Maps
